Transistor

What is a Transistor ?

A Transistor is a smaller semiconductor. It's used to control current or voltage flow in addition to amplifying and generating electrical signals. It may also act as a switch or gate. It ensures the electronic circuit is flowing.

It consists of three layers, or terminals, of semiconductor material, each carrying a current. These transistors can be located embedded in integrated circuits, as they consist of large number of transistors interconnected.

Tranisistors are made up of copper, an insulator such as plastic to warp around the wires , silicone and others.

Tranisistors are combined to design logic gates, where multiple input signals are provided for different outputs

History

The concept of the tranisistor was created long before it's time. In 1947 , John Bardeen and Walter Brattain had successfully created the world's first transistor in Murray Hill, New Jersey.

During the middle of the 20th century, people relied on vacuum tubes to accomplish tasks. They were bulky, fragile and consumed a large amount of power.
