Modern Computers

Historically, computing devices were only used for aiding people in doing tedious calculations. Throughout its evolution however, it rapidly began to become an all-purpose machine that connect people world-wide, no longer becoming a machine used strictly in scientific, or research settings. Computers have advanced into nearly every sector imaginable, such as healthcare, finances, entertainment, education and business. The integration of computers into our society has made it prevalent in our daily lives. However, as the world becomes more dependent on technology, cyber attacks will continue go grow bigger and exploit weaknesses in protection and existing vulnerabilities; often with the end goal of data theft or extortion. Modern computing is thus a double-edged sword; it allows us to host, store, and share information conveniently, but it can also allow for the malicious access and manipulation of such information. As technology continues to advance, it's important to have a balance between its benefits whilst maintaining security.

A Brief History of Cybercrime

When someone says cybercrime, you might think of people living in their basement, sending fake emails to everyone they can find, trying to get your data to sell for a quick buck. This could not be further from the truth.

Cybercrime, over the past decade especially, has become a $1.5T industry with criminal organisations run like legitimate organisations. However, cybercrime is not a new threat. In fact, reports of these attacks go back decades.

Modern Cybercrime Beginnings

The modern history of cybercrime began in 1962, when Allen Scherr, an MIT student, launched a cyber attack against the MIT computer networks, stealing passwords from their database.

See, passwords had been set up to limit the time students had on the computers, so Allan created a punch card that triggered the computer to print all the passwords in the system. He shared these passwords with his friends so that they could get more time on the computer. They also hacked into their teacher's accounts and left taunting messages.

Escalation of Cybercrime

This “attack” would be tamer than most others that would follow. Soon, hackers were stealing tens of millions, then hundreds of millions, of dollars. However, not all hacks were designed for financial gain. Some were designed to wreak havoc, costing businesses and organisations billions.
